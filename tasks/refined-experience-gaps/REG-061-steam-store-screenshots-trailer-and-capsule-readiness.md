## Problem
Store assets are separate from in-app polish. The app can be refined but still underperform if screenshots, trailer beats, capsule art, logo lockup, and feature copy do not communicate the game clearly.

## Target Experience
Steam store materials should show the actual product: board play, mode choice, relic/build depth, daily challenge, cosmetics or meta progress if shipped, and polished UI without misleading features.

## Suggested Implementation
- Define required store screenshots and trailer capture states.
- Use current visual baselines only after `REG-027` refresh and final art tasks.
- Prepare capsule/logo requirements through the asset pipeline.
- Avoid claims about brain training or medical benefits.
- Align feature bullets with shipped systems, not aspirational docs.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Store screenshots use current live UI and final or approved art.
- Trailer plan includes first 10 seconds, gameplay core, depth systems, and call to action.
- Capsule/logo assets meet platform dimensions and rights requirements.
- Store copy does not promise unshipped online, social, or economy features.

## Verification
- Capture store screenshot candidates from deterministic fixtures.
- Review copy against shipped feature list.
- Validate asset dimensions and formats.
